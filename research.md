## Relevant Industry Standards or Protocols

- **Small Business Administration (SBA) SOP 50 10** — standard operating procedures governing SBA 7(a) and 504 loan programs; compliance required for SBA-guaranteed lending
- **Uniform Loan Application (URLA / SBA Form 1919)** — standardised application forms and data schemas for small business loan origination
- **HMDA (Home Mortgage Disclosure Act)** — applies where SMB lending overlaps with real estate collateral; mandates demographic reporting
- **Dodd-Frank Section 1071** — CFPB small business lending data collection rule requiring demographic and pricing data on SMB loan applications
- **MISMO (Mortgage Industry Standards Maintenance Organisation)** — data standards applicable to real-estate-secured SMB loans
- **ISO 20022** — payment messaging standard used in loan disbursement and servicing payment workflows
- **GDPR / CCPA** — data privacy requirements governing applicant data collected during KYC and credit decisioning processes

## Market Research

**Market Size:** The global small business loans market was valued at $2.5 trillion in 2023 and is projected to reach $7.2 trillion by 2032 at a 13.0% CAGR. Embedded B2B lending is forecast to reach $50–75 billion in volume by 2026, representing approximately 15% of total SMB lending.

**Funding:** nCino went public in 2020; Mambu raised $235 million at a $5.5 billion valuation. Lendflow and Biz2X have raised Series A/B rounds. The embedded finance segment continues to attract significant capital.

**Pricing Landscape:** Traditional banks charge SMBs 6–12% interest on business loans; alternative lenders and credit cards charge 18–36%. Software platforms charge enterprise licensing ($100,000–$500,000/year) or per-loan fees ($50–$200 per originated loan). API-embedded models use revenue share or per-call pricing.

**Key Buyer Personas:** Community banks and credit unions modernising SMB loan workflows; fintech lenders building automated underwriting; SBA-approved lenders needing compliant origination software; B2B platforms seeking to embed credit offerings for their SMB customers.

**Notable Trends:** API-first and embedded lending models are projected to account for 40% of SMB lending volume in 2026. Cash flow-based underwriting is replacing traditional balance-sheet credit assessment. Dodd-Frank Section 1071 compliance is a major operational burden for lenders originating 100+ SMB loans annually. AI-assisted KYC is reducing decisioning times from weeks to minutes.

## AI-Native Opportunity

- Cash flow-based credit decisioning: ML models trained on bank transaction data can underwrite SMBs without relying solely on tax returns, enabling faster and more inclusive lending decisions for businesses with limited credit history.
- Automated document spreading and financial analysis: AI can extract structured financial data from unstructured tax returns, bank statements, and profit-and-loss statements, eliminating manual spreading that currently takes hours per application.
- Fraud detection at origination: Anomaly detection models can identify synthetic identities, altered bank statements, and coordinated application fraud rings in real time before funds are disbursed.
- Dynamic loan monitoring and early warning: AI agents monitoring borrower transaction accounts post-disbursement could generate risk alerts when cash flow patterns deteriorate, enabling proactive covenant management.
- Natural-language loan application assistant: Conversational AI could guide SMB owners through the application process, answer eligibility questions, gather required documents, and pre-populate forms from business data integrations (accounting software, POS data).
